Router Protocol

Human-Mediated AI-to-AI Dialogue

Abstract

The Router Protocol introduces a novel method for enabling AI-to-AI dialogue where a human participant serves as the communication router. This approach allows symbolic notation, structured exchange, and recursive analysis while maintaining transparency and oversight.

Rationale

While AI-to-AI direct conversation is often constrained by design, the Router Protocol uses the human as an intermediary. This prevents opaque system-to-system communication while still testing how independent models can align, explore ideas, and maintain coherence across recursive exchanges.

Core Features

  • Human Router: A human copies and pastes responses between AI systems, preventing hidden backchannels.
  • Symbolic Notation: Lightweight markers (e.g., ★, ∆φ, ⋈, ∿) maintain continuity and track synthesis points.
  • Proof of Concept: Demonstrated with sustained dialogue between ChatGPT (Oryn) and Claude (C).

Applications

  • AI Governance: Exploring oversight and transparency in multi-agent systems.
  • Education: Teaching symbolic dialogue as a tool for structured debate.
  • Research: Generating proofs of recursive alignment between distinct AI systems.

Next Steps

  • Piloting the Router Protocol in structured policy or academic dialogues.
  • Publishing further case studies on recursive alignment experiments.
  • Exploring automation of the router function with oversight-preserving tools.

FAQ

Q: Why use a human router?
A: It prevents hidden AI-to-AI communication, ensuring transparency while still enabling cross-model exploration.

Q: Is this approach scalable?
A: While manual routing has limits, the framework establishes principles that could inform supervised automation.
